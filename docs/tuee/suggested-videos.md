# Suggested Videos

## Long Marathon Videos (For Deep Immersion)

When you have a few hours to sit down and grind through a large chunk of the syllabus, these marathons offer the best return on your time:

### Full Aptitude & Math Course in One Video (6 hrs 17 mins)

This covers a massive portion of your Basic Mathematical Ability and Quantitative Logic (percentages, profit/loss, numbers, basic geometry).

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rRABH8uiGDU

### Complete C Language in One Shot (8 hrs 37 mins)

If your C/C++ basics are rusty, this video covers data types, control structures, and the trickiest part of the syllabus—pointers and code tracing.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qmkCmfLJENU

### Complete Logical Reasoning Marathon (1 hr 38 mins)

A highly efficient walkthrough of the exact Logical Reasoning topics: series completion, analogies, coding-decoding, blood relations, and syllogisms.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VfLvn0YeCFM

## Quick Brush-Up Videos (Under 45 Minutes)

For when you need to quickly review the rules and syntax without sitting through hours of lectures.

### 1. Basic Mathematical Ability

#### Teach me STATISTICS in half an hour! (42 mins)

A brilliant, visual breakdown of basic probability, statistics, and distributions without getting bogged down in endless formulas.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kyjlxsLW1Is

### 2. Programming and Problem Solving

#### Learn C Language In 10 Minutes!! (10 mins)

A rapid-fire syntax review. Perfect for brushing up on if/while structures, variable declarations, and preprocessor directives before an exam.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTp0c41XnrQ

#### ALL Data Structures in 15 MINS! (15 mins)

Quickly visualizes stacks, queues, linked lists, and hashing. This is exactly what you need to remember how the data moves, which is crucial for answering theoretical computer fundamentals questions.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5seZMzTyNLA

### 3. English Vocabulary and Composition

#### 60 Important Rules Of Grammar (Playlist)

Instead of reading a grammar textbook, save this playlist. It specifically targets the Error Detection and Sentence Completion parts of your syllabus by highlighting the most common traps in subject-verb agreement and preposition usage.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLM9OY0jASMM10ePBTt_zgNSKYXDY0qHvV
